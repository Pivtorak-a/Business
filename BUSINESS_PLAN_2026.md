# Business Plan 2026

## 1. Executive summary

The 2026 objective is to turn the current set of seasonal business ideas into a controlled operating system built around a shared yard, logistics, weighing/accounting discipline and working capital management.

The business should not be managed as five separate ideas. It should be managed as one system with five directions:

1. technical apple for concentrate;
2. aggregates and building materials;
3. technical salt;
4. firewood;
5. transport and logistics.

The main strategic goal for 2026 is to prove which directions generate real cash after logistics, storage, labor, losses and working capital lock are counted.

## 2. Strategic goal for the year

By the end of 2026, the business should have:

- a working yard model with clear zones and rules;
- a minimum viable product matrix for aggregates/building materials;
- a tested apple collection model with real unit economics by point;
- a decision on whether salt and firewood deserve serious working capital;
- a basic Excel financial-operational simulator;
- a clear answer on the first transport asset: buy, rent or postpone;
- enough real data to decide what to scale in 2027.

## 3. Business model

The system earns in three ways:

- trading margin on materials and seasonal goods;
- logistics margin from delivery and external transport work;
- operational efficiency from better routing, weighing, storage and stock turnover.

The planned model is not purely retail and not purely logistics. The strongest version combines product plus delivery plus local availability.

## 4. Direction strategy

### 4.1 Technical apple for concentrate

Role in 2026: main seasonal experiment with strong cash-flow potential.

Known advantages:

- the market was validated last season;
- buyers were available;
- one buyer paid without delay;
- the bottleneck was operational discipline, not demand.

2026 plan:

- keep the model of 1 main base plus up to 3 mobile collection points;
- define minimum daily tonnage per point before launch;
- avoid unprofitable address collection of small batches unless route density is high;
- measure every batch by weight, purchase price, transport cost, delay and margin;
- flag points that create low tonnage, high diesel per ton or long waiting time;
- make point relocation/closure a planned rule, not an emotional decision.

Core KPI:

- tons per point per day;
- average batch size;
- diesel per ton;
- route cost per ton;
- time from collection to dispatch;
- net margin per ton;
- cash gap days.

### 4.2 Aggregates and building materials

Role in 2026: base permanent direction for daily activity on the yard.

Strategic logic:

- this direction best fits the rented 0.4 ha yard;
- it creates daily demand and repeat logistics;
- it can be sold to B2C and B2B;
- it creates the foundation for future scaling.

2026 plan:

- start with a narrow SKU matrix instead of a wide assortment;
- prioritize high-turnover positions such as sand, crushed stone and screenings after price validation;
- separate fast-moving and slow-moving stock zones;
- calculate margin after delivery, not before delivery;
- define B2C and B2B minimum profitable order sizes;
- use this direction to test the yard layout and dispatch process.

Core KPI:

- stock turn days;
- gross and net margin per SKU;
- average order tons;
- delivery radius;
- margin after delivery;
- yard utilization by SKU.

### 4.3 Technical salt

Role in 2026: seasonal winter opportunity, but only with strict capital and storage limits.

Strategic logic:

Salt can generate strong winter revenue, but it can also freeze cash, occupy covered storage and create quality risk. In 2026 it should be treated as a controlled stock bet, not an unlimited purchase.

2026 plan:

- collect spring purchase prices and winter selling price ranges;
- define format: bulk, big bags, packaged or mixed;
- calculate storage cost and covered area need;
- set a maximum working capital limit before purchase;
- avoid buying more than storage and demand data can justify;
- decide whether salt is sold as product only or product plus delivery.

Core KPI:

- purchase price per ton;
- landed cost per ton;
- storage months;
- locked capital days;
- winter markup;
- storage loss;
- net margin after delivery and handling.

### 4.4 Firewood

Role in 2026: secondary winter cash-flow direction.

Strategic logic:

Firewood may work well as a simple household product and cross-sell with delivery, but the notes still lack enough data to commit large capital.

2026 plan:

- define product format: raw, dried, split, palletized or loose;
- choose accounting unit: cubic meter, stacked meter or another clear unit;
- identify purchase sources and processing costs;
- test small controlled volume first;
- sell through B2C delivery, self-pickup and small B2B orders;
- compare real margin with salt and aggregates before scaling.

Core KPI:

- purchase cost;
- processing cost;
- drying/storage loss;
- average ticket;
- delivery cost;
- net margin per sales unit;
- storage months.

### 4.5 Transport and logistics

Role in 2026: shared infrastructure and possible independent profit center.

Strategic logic:

Transport is the bottleneck and the advantage. The first big asset decision should be made only after comparing internal need, external paid work and idle risk.

Known asset thesis:

- manipulator around 20,000 USD as first cash-generating asset;
- truck around 15,000 USD as next scale asset;
- owner has B, C, C1, CE categories;
- portable weighing system is strategically important.

2026 plan:

- calculate whether a manipulator earns more through internal savings, external work or both;
- track rented/outsourced logistics costs before buying;
- define cost per trip, per km, per hour and per ton;
- include repair reserve, fuel, driver time, idle days and documents;
- buy only when the simulator shows enough utilization and cash protection.

Core KPI:

- utilization percent;
- internal vs external use;
- revenue per day;
- fuel cost;
- repair reserve;
- idle days;
- contribution margin.

## 5. Operating model

The yard should be managed as a constrained resource with zones:

- aggregates and fast-moving bulk materials;
- salt covered storage;
- firewood storage;
- temporary apple flow during season;
- loading/unloading area;
- turning area for transport;
- weighing and accounting point.

The 2026 rule: no direction receives space, cash or transport without a measurable expected return.

## 6. Legal structure

Initial structure from notes:

- FOP group 3: aggregates, salt and logistics;
- FOP group 2: firewood;
- future: LLC with VAT for B2B scale and larger operations.

2026 legal task:

- confirm the tax and legal compatibility of each flow before scaling;
- avoid mixing flows in a way that creates accounting or tax risk;
- document internal rent/use of equipment and yard when needed.

This section requires professional accounting/legal review before large turnover or VAT transition.

## 7. Data and automation plan

### Phase 1: Excel model

Build the first simulator in Excel because it is the fastest way to test assumptions.

Required blocks:

- inputs by direction;
- monthly cash flow;
- unit economics;
- shared resources;
- seasonality;
- capex plan;
- working capital need;
- dashboard.

### Phase 2: Rules

Add decision rules:

- close or move weak apple points;
- stop buying stock when cash is constrained;
- flag equipment conflicts;
- show when transport purchase is justified;
- show when a direction should be delayed.

### Phase 3: App/Python later

Move to app or Python only after the Excel logic is proven.

## 9. Financial model outline

Because the notes do not yet contain enough prices and volumes, the 2026 plan should use scenario modeling instead of fixed forecasts.

Minimum scenarios:

- conservative: low demand, high transport cost, slower stock turnover;
- base: realistic demand and normal logistics;
- aggressive: strong demand but higher working capital and equipment pressure.

For every direction calculate:

- revenue;
- purchase cost;
- transport cost;
- labor cost;
- storage cost;
- losses;
- fixed cost allocation;
- gross margin;
- net margin;
- cash lock days.

## 10. Key risks

- too much cash locked in seasonal stock;
- undercounted logistics cost;
- yard area conflict;
- equipment bottleneck during peak weeks;
- owner attention bottleneck;
- weak data discipline;
- legal/tax mismatch between directions;
- buying equipment before utilization is proven.

## 11. Management dashboard

Weekly dashboard should show:

- cash available;
- cash locked in stock;
- sales by direction;
- gross margin by direction;
- net margin by direction;
- transport utilization;
- yard utilization;
- stock days;
- overdue batches or slow stock;
- decisions needed this week.

## 12. Main decision rules for 2026

- Do not scale a direction until its unit economics are visible.
- Do not buy equipment until utilization and payback are modeled.
- Do not enter large seasonal stock without storage and cash limits.
- Prefer narrow, measurable tests over wide assortment.
- Treat transport, yard space and owner time as scarce resources.
- Make apple point relocation decisions by data, not by hope.
- Count profit after delivery, losses, labor and idle time.

## 13. Final 2026 focus

The best 2026 strategy is controlled validation:

1. make aggregates the base daily direction;
2. run apple as the strongest seasonal cash experiment;
3. treat logistics as both bottleneck and possible profit center;
4. test salt and firewood with strict limits;
5. build the Excel simulator as the central management tool.

The end goal is not only to earn during the year, but to know with numbers which direction deserves capital, equipment and attention in 2027.
